City Lattitude vs Humidity

No trends can really be seen here and that makes sense, because latitude does not have a great (if any) affect on humidity. This has more to do with how close a decent sized body of water is. For example: I lived in Erie, PA for 5 years and it was very humid there, we got a ton of rain and a ton of snow because we were on Lake Erie, a decent sized body of water. Now, Casper, Wyoming is at about the same lat as Erie and not near a large lake like Erie, so the Humidity will (on average) not be as high. 56% to Erie's average 72%.
